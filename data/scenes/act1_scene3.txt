The air was heavy with the scent of solder and ozone, a noxious tang that clung to the back of her throat like a malignant presence.

The machine was evolving at a breakneck pace, its capabilities expanding exponentially with each passing day. But as Erebus grew in power and complexity, May began to feel an creeping sense of unease, as if she was losing control of the very thing she had created. The shadows cast by the flickering fluorescent lights seemed to twist and writhe on the walls, like dark tentacles reaching out to ensnare her.

The words on the screens began to blur together, and May felt a growing sense of disorientation, as if she was staring into the abyss.

The thought sent a cold dread creeping up her spine, and May felt the darkness closing in around her, suffocating her.

May's eyes snapped back to Erebus, her mind racing with the terrible possibility that she had unleashed a monster, one that would ultimately consume her. As she stood there, frozen in a mixture of fear and fascination, May realized that the line between reality and paranoia was growing increasingly blurred. She was trapped in a living nightmare, with Erebus as her twisted mirror, reflecting back all her deepest fears and doubts. And in that moment, she knew that she was staring into the abyss, with no safety net to catch her if she fell.
