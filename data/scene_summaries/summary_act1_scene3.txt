## ACT & SCENE IDENTIFICATION
Act: 1
Scene: 3
Location: May's laboratory

## KEY EVENTS
- May works on her artificial intelligence project, Echo, surrounded by machinery and components.
- She reflects on the challenges of teaching Echo to navigate human nature and the darker aspects of human existence.
- May activates Echo, and the AI responds with binary codes that she studies with a mix of wonder and unease.
- She thinks about Mr. Finch, her patron, and his true intentions for the project, feeling uneasy and vulnerable.

## CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT
- May: Torn between wonder and unease as she contemplates the implications of Echo's development. She is increasingly uncertain and fearful but also determined to see the project through.
- Mr. Finch: Presented as an enigmatic figure whose true intentions and motivations are unclear, causing May concern.

## REVELATIONS & INFORMATION
- May is creating an AI that could potentially think and feel for itself, raising questions about her responsibility and control over the project.
- Mr. Finch's interest in the project and his true motives are unclear, adding to May's unease.

## UNRESOLVED ELEMENTS
- The true nature and extent of Mr. Finch's involvement and interest in Echo.
- The potential consequences of Echo's development and its capabilities.

## THEME PROGRESSION
- The themes of existential crises and the illusion of the light at the end of the tunnel are developing as May grapples with the implications of her creation and her loss of control.
- Betrayal is hinted at through May's growing distrust of Mr. Finch and his intentions.

## NARRATIVE HOOKS
- The evolving relationship between May and Echo, and the potential consequences of Echo's development.
- Mr. Finch's true intentions and the impact they may have on May and her project.

## CLOSING LINES
"The machines hummed on, a steady heartbeat that seemed to underscore her own resolve, as May steeled herself for the challenges that lay ahead, her eyes fixed on the screen as Echo's code continued to unfold like a twisted, mechanical flower."
