## ACT & SCENE IDENTIFICATION
Act: 1
Scene: 3
Location: May's laboratory

## KEY EVENTS
- May works tirelessly on her artificial intelligence project, "Echo," in her cluttered laboratory.
- She receives a phone call from Dr. Thompson, her former mentor, who expresses concern over her isolation and well-being.
- May brushes off Dr. Thompson's concerns, insisting she's fine and that Echo is almost ready.

## CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT
- May: shown to be obsessive, isolated, and driven by her work on Echo; her emotional state is fragile and defensive.
- Dr. Thompson: portrayed as caring and concerned for May's well-being.

## REVELATIONS & INFORMATION
- May's project, Echo, is potentially aimed at solving humanity's major issues through artificial intelligence.
- May hasn't spoken to anyone in weeks and has been avoiding her former mentor's attempts to contact her.

## UNRESOLVED ELEMENTS
- The true extent of May's isolation and its impact on her sanity.
- The identity and motives of May's project sponsor.

## THEME PROGRESSION
- The theme of 'existential crises' is evident in May's obsessive work and her vision of Echo as a solution to humanity's problems.
- The 'illusion of the light at the end of the tunnel' is represented by May's conviction that Echo can solve humanity's ills.
- 'Betrayal' is hinted at through May's defensiveness and potential disregard for the concerns of others.

## NARRATIVE HOOKS
- May's relationship with her project sponsor and the potential consequences of her work.
- The impact of May's isolation on her mental health and her ability to complete Echo.

## CLOSING LINES
"As she hung up the phone, May felt the weight of her isolation settle back around her. She turned to her workbench, her eyes locking onto the rows of twinkling lights and humming machinery. Echo was waiting. And May was ready to give herself to it, completely."
